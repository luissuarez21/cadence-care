CADENCE RISK RUBRIC — Preeclampsia surveillance (ok | monitor | escalate | escalate_urgent)

Purpose
-------
The single source of truth for what each risk level MEANS and the plain-English
language used to explain it. The deterministic classifier (backend/risk/classifier.py)
implements these exact thresholds; the LLM-as-judge (backend/eval/arize_judge.py) uses
this rubric to evaluate whether an escalation was appropriate. Keep all three aligned.

Thresholds (ACOG preeclampsia criteria)
---------------------------------------
- Severe-range BP:   systolic >= 160 OR diastolic >= 110 (a single reading)
- Elevated BP:       systolic >= 140 OR diastolic >= 90
- Severe headache:   headache_severity >= 7 (0-10 scale)
- Moderate headache: headache_severity 4-6
- Vision changes:    blurred vision, spots, or flashing lights
- Facial/hand swelling: swelling in the face or hands (not feet/ankles alone)
- Decreased fetal movement: reported less than the patient's baseline
- Aspirin nonadherence: low-dose aspirin missed 2+ consecutive check-ins

Severity ordering (worst wins): escalate_urgent > escalate > monitor > ok.
When multiple signals are present, the highest severity governs.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
escalate_urgent — urgent warning signs; notify the OB immediately
- Severe-range BP on any single reading (>=160/110), OR
- Severe headache (>=7/10) TOGETHER WITH vision changes (eclampsia warning), OR
- Regular contractions before 37 weeks (possible preterm labor).
Rationale voice (clinician): name the exact reading/symptom and that it meets the
care plan's urgent threshold. Action: "Contact the patient now; consider same-day
clinical evaluation."

escalate — above threshold; notify the OB, advance the appointment if warranted
- Two elevated readings (>=140/90) on the same day, OR
- Severe persistent headache (>=7/10) alone, OR
- New vision changes alone, OR
- New facial/hand swelling WITH an elevated BP reading, OR
- Decreased/absent fetal movement, OR
- Upper-abdominal / right-upper-quadrant pain (if reported).
Rationale voice: state the readings/symptoms plainly and that they meet the plan's
notify threshold. Action: "Review today and reach out; consider advancing the visit."

monitor — worth watching, not yet an escalation
- A single elevated reading (>=140/90) on its own, OR
- A moderate headache (4-6/10), OR
- Facial/hand swelling with normal BP, OR
- Rapid weight gain (>=5 lb / 2.3 kg in a week, if available), OR
- Aspirin missed 2+ consecutive check-ins.
Rationale voice: note the finding and that one such reading is worth watching but
not yet a two-reading / urgent escalation. Action: "No action needed now — keep an
eye on this at the next check-in."

ok — everything within the care plan's normal range
Rationale voice: "All readings on the latest check-in are within the care plan's
normal range." Action: "Continue routine monitoring."

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Rationale rules (apply to every level)
- Plain English, no black box. Always name the actual numbers/symptoms that drove it.
- Never diagnose or name a condition. Describe findings vs. thresholds, not diseases.
- Patient-facing copy stays calm and routes to the OB; clinical detail is for the
  dashboard, not the patient.
